Atelier redesigned — "Floating Pastel"

Catppuccin's segmented panel was too quiet. Replaced with floating pastel pills — every segment is its own fully-rounded colored pill with visible gaps between them. Glacier (cascade) and Bauhaus (plates) unchanged.

Three distinct silhouettes now:
Glacier — connected cascade (touching pills, diagonal notches)
Atelier — floating beads (separated rounded pills, varied pastel colors)
Bauhaus — brutalist plates (separated outlined boxes, sharp corners)

What's new

  • Every segment is a self-contained pill with fully-rounded ends and visible gaps between them. Reads as a chain of beads, not a continuous bar.
  • Three pill styles for visual rhythm:
    • Solid pastel fills (session=mauve, path=lavender, branch=peach, time=pink) — the "loud" pills
    • Subtle surface0 fills (window list) — the "quiet" pills with mauve marker for active
    • Colored outlines (stats: lavender CPU, teal RAM, yellow battery) — the "ghost" pills
  • Six different pastel colors active at once — mauve, lavender, peach, teal, yellow, pink — much richer than the previous Atelier's single mauve+overlay.
  • Pink time pill closes the chain, balancing the mauve session pill on the left (visual rhyme)
  • No cascade, no continuous block — every pill stands alone, distinct from Glacier's flowing pattern
