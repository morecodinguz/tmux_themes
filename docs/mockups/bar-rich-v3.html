Three Designs, Three Patterns

Each theme gets its own structural identity, not just a different palette of the same shape. You should be able to identify which theme is active from across the room without reading.

The structural language of each:
Glacier · tmux2kCascading Powerline — pills abutting via diagonal notches, color flows L→R, framed stats
Atelier · catppuccinSegmented Panel — one solid focal pill on left, one long elegant capsule on right with sub-items inside
Bauhaus · ohmytmuxBrutalist Plates — heavy session frame, individual outlined data-plates on right, no pills/cascades

Identity: segmented panel

  • One solid focal pill on left (mauve session, fully rounded). One long capsule on right (surface0 bg, fully rounded) containing every metric as a sub-segment.
  • No cascade — there are exactly two heavy elements with a quiet middle between them. Asymmetric weight balance.
  • Inside the right capsule: each metric has its colored icon + value, separated by dim middle dots
  • Window list stays light (just text + dot separators) — the pastel calm is preserved while the bar still feels designed
  • Distinct from Glacier's "row of touching pills" — this is "two endpoints, one quiet middle"

Identity: brutalist plates

  • Triple-block heavy session frame ▌▌▌ ▐▐▐ — even heavier than v2's double block. Rosewater bg, black text, max contrast.
  • No cascade. No pills. Right side is a row of thin outlined "data plates" — each metric in its own rosewater-bordered rectangle, like industrial labels stamped onto machinery.
  • Window list uses ▶ marker for active (instead of ●) and ▎ block separators between items — keeps the brutalist character with vertical-bar motifs throughout
  • Right-side icons hold the warm color signal (red branch, peach battery, white clock, rosewater date)
  • Distinct from Atelier's "single capsule" and Glacier's "cascade" — this is "isolated framed boxes"
